# Crypto Fees Explained: Gas, Network Fees, Slippage, and Hidden Costs

Fees can be the most confusing part of crypto for beginners. From Ethereum gas to Solana transaction costs, plus hidden exchange and swap fees, understanding them is crucial to avoid surprises.

---

## 1. Ethereum Gas Fees

- Gas is a unit measuring computational effort  
- Fees fluctuate based on network demand  
- High-demand times = higher fees  

> Tip: Start with small transfers to get a feel for the network. Learn wallet setup here: [Set Up Your First Wallet](/blog/getting-started/set-up-first-wallet/)

---

## 2. Solana and Other Low-Fee Chains

- Solana is cheaper and faster  
- Good for memecoin launches and frequent transactions  
- Always check wallet confirmations

---

## 3. Exchange Fees

- Withdrawal fees vary per platform  
- Swap fees hidden in rates/slippage  
- Bridge fees apply for cross-chain moves

---

## 4. Slippage Explained

- Difference between expected and executed price  
- Common in low-liquidity tokens like new memecoins  
- Minimize by adjusting slippage tolerance in wallets/exchanges

> Learn tools to track and manage fees during memecoin launches: [Tools](/tools)

---

## 5. Hidden Costs

- Approval transactions for smart contracts  
- Layer 2 fees  
- Transaction retries for failed transfers

---

## 6. Beginner Takeaways

- Always double-check fees before confirming  
- Start small and track cost impact  
- Plan for memecoin spikes and network congestion

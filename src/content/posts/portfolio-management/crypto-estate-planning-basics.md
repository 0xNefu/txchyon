## What Is Crypto Estate Planning?

Crypto estate planning is the practice of **ensuring your digital assets can be securely passed to heirs or beneficiaries** in case of death or incapacity.

Without proper planning, access to your crypto can be lost forever.

> Estate planning is the final layer of **portfolio management**, securing both wealth and legacy.

## Why Crypto Estate Planning Matters

Crypto is digital and irreversible. Common pitfalls:
- Losing private keys  
- Forgetting wallets  
- No clear inheritance instructions  

Proper estate planning:
- Protects heirs  
- Reduces legal conflicts  
- Preserves wealth efficiently

## How Estate Planning Ties Into Portfolio Management

- **Allocation:** ensure heirs inherit proportional shares of BTC, ETH, altcoins, and stablecoins  
- **Risk Management:** use secure wallets and backups to avoid permanent loss  
- **Performance Tracking:** document holdings and cost basis for heirs  
- **Tax-Loss Harvesting:** plan for potential inheritance taxes or capital gains for beneficiaries  

> Understanding the full portfolio context makes estate planning easier and more effective.

---

## Step-by-Step: Setting Up a Crypto Estate Plan

1. **List All Assets**  
   Include exchange accounts, wallets, staking, and DeFi positions.

2. **Choose Access Methods**  
   Multi-sig wallets, hardware wallets, and legal documentation.

3. **Draft Legal Instructions**  
   Work with an attorney familiar with digital assets.

4. **Communicate With Heirs**  
   Make sure trusted individuals know how to access and use the assets.

5. **Update Regularly**  
   Review at least annually or after major portfolio changes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can crypto be left in a will?
Yes, but ensure keys and access instructions are included.

### Are trusts necessary for small portfolios?
Not always, but they provide security and can avoid probate.

### How often should I review my estate plan?
At least once per year or after significant portfolio changes.
